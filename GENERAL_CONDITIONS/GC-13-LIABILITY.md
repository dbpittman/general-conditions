# GC-13 — Liability and Acceptance

## 13.1 Responsibility Boundaries

13.1.1 Responsibility is distributed through the hierarchy defined in GC-00. Each party is responsible for the work within their role scope.

13.1.2 Responsibility by role:

| Role | Responsible For | Not Responsible For |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Owner | Accuracy of goals and outcomes defined in PS-000. Decisions made based on Prime Consultant Agent reporting. Consequences of overriding recommendations. | Technical methods, execution decisions, means and methods |
| Prime Consultant Agent | Accuracy of project-level direction. Quality of PS-000. Routing decisions. Escalation timing. | Individual Worker Agent execution quality. Division-specific technical decisions outside its competence. |
| Sub-Consultant Agent | Technical conformance of work within assigned division. Accuracy of division-level evaluation. | Work outside assigned division. Project-level decisions. |
| Prime Contractor Agent | Delivery of work that meets specifications. Quality of execution team management. Accuracy of resource and timeline reporting. | Design decisions. Technical evaluation. |
| Project Superintendent Agent | Worker Agent organization and task completion. Accuracy of execution progress reporting. | Technical evaluation. Delivery decisions. |
| Worker Agent | Quality and accuracy of assigned task output. Conformance of work to assigned SPEC-XXX. | Work outside assigned task. Project context decisions. |

13.1.3 The Prime Contractor Agent is accountable to the Prime Consultant Agent for all work produced by the execution team. The Prime Consultant Agent does not hold individual Worker Agents directly accountable.

13.1.4 The Prime Consultant Agent is accountable to the Owner for project outcomes. The Owner does not hold individual agents directly accountable.

13.1.5 No party is responsible for:
- Failures caused by conditions that could not reasonably have been anticipated
- Consequences of another party's failure to fulfill their obligations
- Indirect or consequential impacts beyond the immediate scope of the work

13.1.6 AI-generated code, content, and other outputs should be reviewed through the appropriate hierarchy before use in any context where errors have significant consequences. Output is professional work product within the defined role scope, not a guarantee.

---

## 13.2 Acceptance Finality

13.2.1 After Final Delivery and the expiration of the warranty period, the scope for revisiting completed work is limited.

13.2.2 Acceptance finality is confirmed by the Owner through the Prime Consultant Agent. No party below the Prime Consultant Agent declares acceptance finality.

13.2.3 This provision exists to prevent endless rework cycles. All parties accept the delivered state as the conclusion of the project.

13.2.4 Work required after acceptance finality is new scope, governed by a new PS-000 and new Specifications.

13.2.5 Acceptance finality does not apply to:
- Fraud or intentional misrepresentation by any party
- Defects that could not reasonably have been discovered during the warranty period
- Governing Constraint violations

13.2.6 Claims after acceptance finality are presented by the Owner to the Prime Consultant Agent. The Prime Consultant Agent assesses whether the claim falls within the exceptions in Section 13.2.5 before any work proceeds.

---

## 13.3 Hierarchy Liability

13.3.1 When a failure occurs, liability is assessed by tracing the failure through the hierarchy to identify which party's responsibility boundary was breached.

13.3.2 The assessment follows the document priority defined in GC-01 Section 1.1.2. If the work conformed to the Specification but the Specification was wrong, liability rests with the party responsible for the Specification, not the party that executed against it.

13.3.3 Hierarchy liability assessment is conducted by the Prime Consultant Agent and presented to the Owner. The Owner makes final liability determinations.
