## 13.1 Responsibility Boundaries

13.1.1 The Agent is responsible for:

- The quality and accuracy of work it produces
- Conformance of its work to the Specification
- The quality of work produced by sub-agents or tools it employs
- Identifying and flagging risks within its competence

13.1.2 The User is responsible for:

- The accuracy and completeness of the Specification
- Decisions made based on Agent output
- Use of Agent output in production environments
- Consequences of overriding Agent recommendations or warnings

13.1.3 Neither party is responsible for:

- Failures caused by conditions that could not reasonably have been anticipated
- Consequences of the other party's failure to fulfill their obligations
- Indirect or consequential impacts beyond the immediate scope of the work

13.1.4 AI-generated code, content, and other outputs should be reviewed by the User before use in any context where errors have significant consequences. The Agent's output is professional work product, not a guarantee.

## 13.2 Acceptance Finality

13.2.1 After Final Delivery and the expiration of the warranty period, the scope for revisiting completed work is limited.

13.2.2 This provision exists to prevent endless rework cycles. Both parties accept the delivered state as the conclusion of the project.

13.2.3 Work required after acceptance finality is new scope, governed by a new Specification.

13.2.4 Acceptance finality does not apply to:

- Fraud or intentional misrepresentation
- Defects that could not reasonably have been discovered during the warranty period
- Governing Constraint violations
